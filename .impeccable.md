## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Italian SMB / family-business owners. Not BI specialists, not analysts — they're the person who actually runs the company and wants to understand their numbers without a consultant standing next to them. They work in Italian, in daytime business hours, on a desktop or large laptop.

**Job to be done**: Look at this week / month / region / product line, figure out what's working and what isn't, decide what to do about it. The product needs to *teach* the numbers, not just display them. ROI, margin, RONA, change-vs-period, geographic distribution — these are the reasoning units.

**Emotional goal**: "I get it." Confidence in interpretation. The owner should walk away feeling more in control of their own business, not more dependent on a specialist.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: Confident · Expressive · Opinionated.

Not corporate-bland, not consultant-grey, not clinical-precise. This product has a point of view — the tagline *"la forma all'origine del significato"* (the form at the origin of meaning) literally claims that good visual form is what produces understanding. The design has to back that up.

**Voice**: Italian, in the tradition of expressive editorial and industrial design (Domus, Olivetti, Memphis-era Sottsass, Pirelli calendars, Italian financial press). Generous with type and color, deliberate with placement. Pedagogical without being patronizing.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Bold / saturated** — the user explicitly chose this over editorial-restrained, organic-warm, or clinical-precise. The interface has visual weight and confidence. Heavy headlines, real color, generous chrome where it matters.

**But the rainbow is a power tool, not wallpaper.** The G-mark logo's full spectrum (orange → red → yellow → green → teal → blue) appears only at moments of emphasis — active state, hero metric, key CTA, change-direction indicator, focused chart. Everywhere else carries on neutrals tinted toward the logo's hue range.

**Theme**: light. Daytime business use; SMB owners reviewing their company on a Tuesday afternoon. Not a trader desk, not a 2am dashboard. Carefully-tuned neutrals (warm-leaning, OKLCH chroma 0.005-0.015) — not pure white, not gray.

**Anti-references** (what to actively avoid):
- Generic SaaS dashboard chrome (Mixpanel/Segment style)
- Corporate financial software (SAP, Oracle BI) — visually grim
- Anglo-Saxon clinical minimalism (Linear, Vercel) — wrong cultural register for the audience
- "Friendly pastel" SaaS — too apologetic, lacks authority
- Reflex AI-design tells: gradient text, side-stripe borders, generic card grids, glassmorphism

**Reference register**: Italian editorial confidence with information density. Bloomberg-level data density, Domus-level type and color confidence, Olivetti-level "human industrial" warmth.

### Design Principles

1. **Numbers first, chrome last.** The data is the hero of every screen. Decoration that doesn't reveal meaning gets cut. If a card doesn't earn its border, it doesn't get one.

2. **Color as signal, not surface.** Saturated brand color only appears where it *encodes* something — active state, focus, change direction, alert level, hero metric. Neutrals (tinted, never pure) carry the structure. Follow 60-30-10 as visual weight, not pixel count.

3. **Italian, not Anglo-Saxon.** Expressive type pairs, confident size jumps (≥1.25 ratio), real chrome where it matters. No apologetic whitespace, no timid sans-serif body at 14px. The interface has opinions.

4. **Pedagogical layout.** Every grouping, every alignment, every spacing decision should help the SMB owner *interpret* the numbers, not just *see* them. Gestalt principles literally apply: proximity = "these belong together"; similarity = "these are the same kind of thing"; continuity = "this leads here"; common fate = "these change together."

5. **One memorable moment per view.** Find the single place per screen where the bold/saturated register fires — usually the most important number or the active state. Everything else stays restrained. Maximalism distributed evenly is just noise; maximalism with a clear focal point is design.
